A puff of smoke

5 minute read, creative piece
Written by Aj Xiang


I am born as the match strikes the box. Sparks fly, smoke curls in tight spirals, as I leap out into a world of darkness. As the fumes dissipate, wafting through the air in carefree ecstasy, I explode in a parade of flames. I am fire, newly born; easily vanquished with a puff of air, but deadly if uncared for. I bring life, cure death and fight against the insane suffocation of darkness, but it all comes at a price: Feed me.

As I whittle away at this tiny stick of dry wood, ten faces watch me with intense anticipation. The one who struck the match has a face of pleading desperateness; her visage is wrenched with insurmountable agony and concentration. Her shivering hands lower me into a pyramid of damp and sodden sticks. A small pile of broken matches lie beside her heads burnt black with failure. With her other hand, she clenches the matchbox until its crushed into a pile of ripped cardboard and lost hope.

Snow has already begun to fall serenely against the bitter wind. It whines and cries, like a massive wounded creature. The dense fog has closed the shutters of the evening sun. The cold air takes bites at me, clawing and spitting. Yet I burn hotter than ever, unlike my predecessors who lack spirit, strength, and power to face sleet.

I launch myself into the nearest leaf, cackling and crackling into the dark night sky. Consuming this damp leaf, I burrow further into the sodden recesses of the branch. The moisture kicks back and I recoil in shock, spitting out smoke in indignation. I’m reduced to an ember, a tiny glowing tip. Yet I persistently hold on to my fading life. Every face around me groans and screams in dismay.

A soft, fibrous substance is pushed into me from every side. Gratefully devouring the cotton, I climb and pull myself up into a new burrow of leaves. Nurtured, coddled, and loved, I grow into massive, unstoppable flame. Spitting out smoke in nauseated displeasure, I combat the sleet that shoot like bullets through the chaos of a sky. Every face lights up in hope and pleasure, growing red. My warmth flows like running water through their bodies. Yet even as they laugh and cheer, a mountain top gust of wind topples me from my fiery throne.

Pain. It clutches at my body and runs like molten lead through my veins. Every move is a step into severe agony. The freezing, merciless air licks at my skin and cuts at the very branches that hold my life. Rain gouges my body until I become aimless, wandering smoke, drifting into the turmoil of the night air. I scream in raging anger at this premature death. The more I cling to this disaster of my life, the more the wind pulls at me to drift away. ‘Give up,’ it says,’You have no meaning.’

However, I have embodied smoke, the angry ashes of dead fire. Free and not bound to dry leaves and wood, I wander through the air, yelling in joy. Spiralling up, swooping down, billowing through trees and rocks. The chains of heat and oxygen break as I lie in the clouds. The pinpricks of tiny stars shine persistently through the peaceful night, while I float up and up. I now dance with the stars.
