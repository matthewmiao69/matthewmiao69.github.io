Hello

3 minute read, Poem
Written by Tony Lang

Hello,
Hello,
Hello,
Can you hear me?
Are my words managing to seep out?

I walk to the train.
A sea of grey passes on my left
and on my right
and in front of me.
The desolate noise of city life engulfs me.
But it’s okay.
I smile and things move on.

Hello,
Hello,
Hello,
Can you hear me?
Are my pleas managing to seep out?

The game is over.
We lost.
My team walks by me,
their expressions sour like a pufferfish gone wrong.
As we enter the bus I can’t
stop
thinking.
This entire loss was my fault.
But it’s okay.
I drop my stick and smile.
I hope this feeling won’t be here for long.

Hello,
Hello,
Hello,
Can you hear me?
Are my worries managing to seep out?

Lunchtime arrives like a
bullet,
so sharp and precise.
Everyone hustling like ducks
going out,
leaving the confused
helpless
duckling behind.
But it’s okay.
There’s always a happy ending.
Hello?
Hello?
Are my words…

All I see is red.
I feel cheated.
I feel robbed.
This injustice cannot go on.
As I angrily slam away the world from my ears,
music rewatering my soul,
my friends look and they laugh
and I cry dry tears.
(And I laugh instead)

Hello?

Another day passes
and I can’t
stop
thinking.

Hello?

All of this is my fault.

HELLO?

And I’ll take it till I die.
